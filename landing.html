Relay Console

A local, offline, single‑file HTML tool for relaying a question or task between ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and other AI assistants, by hand.

You copy each prompt out and paste each answer back. Relay Console keeps the multi‑model conversation organized: the role framing, the running transcript, and the final synthesis. Nothing runs on its own.

How it works

You control every handoff

Relay Console never contacts a model on its own. Every handoff is yours: it frames the prompt, you carry it to the chatbot, you bring the answer back. The tool keeps track so a four‑way exchange stays legible instead of scattered across tabs.

  1. Write one question and choose the assistants.
  2. Relay Console builds a prompt with role framing and the relevant context.
  3. Copy the prompt into the selected chatbot, then paste the answer back.
  4. Continue through debate or blind‑comparison mode, then synthesize the results.

Why use it

Local and offline

Open the saved HTML file in your browser. There is no server and no account; the tool runs entirely on your machine.

Human-in-the-loop

You decide what gets copied, pasted, trimmed, skipped, or synthesized at every step. Nothing moves without you.

No API orchestration

Relay Console does not call model APIs or drive chatbot tabs. It organizes the manual relay; the carrying across is yours.

What Relay Console is not

✕An autonomous agent frameworkIt takes no action on its own and makes no calls for you.
✕A browser extensionNothing to install, no permissions, no access to your tabs.
✕A hosted AI workspaceNo accounts and no servers. The file is the whole product.
✕A tab automation toolIt does not type into, read, or control other sites.

Relay Console itself runs in your browser and does not upload your questions, answers, or sessions. When you choose to open an assistant, your browser opens that provider's own site; anything you paste there is handled under that provider's terms.
